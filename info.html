Mathematics Behind the game

It must have come to one's notice that the chances of winning a car became significantly higher when the choice was changed rather than keeping the same choice,but why is it so? The reason can be explained with conditional probability theorem.

Conditional probability

Conditioned probability as the name suggests is probability conditioned according to an event,in other words it defines how the probability of an event occurring is defined provided that another event has already occurred.Let's take an example, on a given day what is the probability that there are clouds in the sky ? Practically speaking we can estimate the chances by taking into account the weather history of the area, the season ,whether there are clouds in the neighbouring areas or not. Mathematically there is no way to provide an accurate probability of such event. Now let's say another event has been confirmed to occur,it is raining in the area , now certainly we can calculate probability of clouds being in the sky as 1 as it is not possible for rain to occur without clouds.

Mathematically given an event B , probability of event A is calculated as probability of events A and B occurring together divided by the probability of event B.

How does conditional probability work in this game?

To demonstrate how switching the choice maximises chances of victory we will calculate probability of winning in both cases whether we switch our choice or not and compare the probability of winning in both the cases.

Not Switching

P(Winning)=A*C + B*D
A = 1/3, B=2/3
C = 1(Car was selected and door not switched)
D = 0(Goat selected and door not switched)
Hence probability of winning = 1/3 = 33.33%

Switching

P(Winning)=A*C + B*D
A = 1/3, B=2/3
C = 0(Car was selected and door switched)
D = 1(Goat selected and door switched)
Hence probability of winning = 2/3 = 66.66%

This concludes how probability of winning increases when door is switched
